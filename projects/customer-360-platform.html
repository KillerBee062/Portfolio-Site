01 / Context

The problem.

Customer data lived in 12 separate systems — CRM, e-commerce, loyalty, pharmacy POS, service tickets, marketing automation. The same person could exist as 4 different records across systems, with no reliable way to link them. Campaigns double-targeted; service agents couldn't see the full history; marketing attribution was guesswork.

02 / Approach

How it works.

A probabilistic identity resolution pipeline that builds a graph of customer entities and links records via deterministic + fuzzy matching. dbt models build the canonical customer table; Spark jobs handle the graph stitching nightly.

  • Ingestion: Source CDC streams into Snowflake; dbt staging models standardise schemas.
  • Matching: Spark-based graph resolution using email/phone/address hashing + name similarity.
  • Survivorship: Rules decide which source wins per attribute; provenance preserved.
  • Consumption: Reverse-ETL into CRM, marketing automation and service tools.
NDA: Matching rules, client identity and exact data shape are abstracted.
03 / Outcomes

What shipped.

  • Collapsed 7.8M source records into 4.2M unified profiles with 94% match accuracy.
  • Cut campaign double-targeting by ~31%, saving annualised media spend.
  • Service agents now see full cross-channel history in one screen.
  • Foundation for downstream personalisation and propensity models.
