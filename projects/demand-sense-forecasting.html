01 / Context

The problem.

The business was relying on simple moving-average forecasts that broke down at the SKU-region level. Stockouts averaged 8% during promotional weeks while excess inventory sat in warehouses for slow-moving categories. Planners spent more time reconciling spreadsheets than making decisions.

We needed a forecasting engine that could ingest weather, holidays, promotions and macro signals — and produce a forecast planners could trust at the daily-SKU-store grain.

02 / Approach

How it works.

Hierarchical LightGBM models per category, with reconciliation across product-region pyramids. Features include lagged sales, calendar effects, regional weather (temperature anomalies, rainfall), promotion calendars, and competitor pricing signals.

  • Ingestion: Airflow DAGs pull from POS, ERP, weather APIs and promo systems into Snowflake.
  • Modelling: One LightGBM model per category-region cluster, hyperparameter-tuned weekly.
  • Reconciliation: Hierarchical forecasts reconciled via MinT for consistency across aggregations.
  • Tracking: MLflow logs every model run; backtests trigger automatic retraining when drift exceeds threshold.
NDA: Specific feature engineering, business rules and client identity are abstracted in this case study.
03 / Outcomes

What shipped.

  • Reduced stockouts from 8% to 6.2% (a 22% relative improvement) within 2 quarters of go-live.
  • Released $3.4M in trapped working capital by trimming safety stock on stable SKUs.
  • Cut planner forecast-prep time from 12 hours/week to 90 minutes.
  • Model now drives weekly S&OP discussions across 6 business units.
