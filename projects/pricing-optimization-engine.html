01 / Context

The problem.

Pricing decisions across the consumer portfolio were anchored on cost-plus rules, with quarterly manual reviews. Elasticities were assumed, not measured. Promotions ran at -15% by default with no evidence the discount was needed to move volume.

02 / Approach

How it works.

  • Causal estimation: Double-ML and uplift trees estimate price elasticity per SKU-segment, controlling for confounders.
  • Constrained optimisation: Maximise margin subject to volume floors, competitive ceilings and brand guard-rails.
  • Champion-challenger: Every recommendation runs as a controlled A/B before full rollout.
  • Governance: Category managers approve final prices in a review UI — model proposes, humans dispose.
NDA: Specific elasticities, SKUs and pricing rules are abstracted.
03 / Outcomes

What shipped.

  • Gross margin up 3.1 percentage points on optimised categories vs. control.
  • Volume held flat to +1% — no cannibalisation.
  • Promo depth reduced by 4pp on average; ROI on remaining promos doubled.
  • Now part of the quarterly pricing committee's standard toolkit.
