01 / Context

The problem.

The logistics team was reconciling fuel cards and trip sheets weekly — catching abuse and route deviations only after the fact. Vehicle telematics existed but data lived in a vendor portal nobody opened. By the time a problem surfaced, the fuel was already gone.

02 / Approach

How it works.

  • Ingestion: Vehicle MQTT streams ingested via Kafka — GPS, fuel, RPM, door open events at 1Hz.
  • Stream processing: Flink jobs apply per-vehicle baselines + rule library for fuel/idle/route checks.
  • Storage: TimescaleDB stores raw + aggregates; hot data hits dashboards instantly.
  • Alerting: Anomalies push to fleet ops Slack with vehicle, driver and last-known-location.
NDA: Vehicle types, baseline rules and client identity are abstracted.
03 / Outcomes

What shipped.

  • Fuel cost down 14% in the first quarter after rollout.
  • Mean time-to-detect for route deviation: 28 seconds.
  • Eliminated weekly trip-sheet reconciliation — fleet team redirected to route planning.
  • Foundation for predictive maintenance work now in progress.
